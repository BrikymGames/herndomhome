Mission & Vision

Preserving history, inspiring community, and opening doors for the future.

Our Mission

Why We Exist

The Herndon Home Museum preserves and interprets the legacy of the Herndon family — their contributions to African American enterprise, education, and community leadership — for the benefit of present and future generations.

We serve as a beacon of inspiration, demonstrating what is possible through determination, craftsmanship, and a commitment to uplifting others.

Our Vision

Where We're Headed

Under a new Board of Trustees, The Herndon Foundation has embarked on a strategic redesign to enhance its impact in the community. The re-opening of the Herndon Home is the first step in this process.

We envision a vibrant museum that welcomes all — a place where students discover history, scholars conduct research, families create memories, and the community gathers to celebrate African American heritage in Atlanta and beyond.

"A Beacon of Inspiration to All"

The Trustees are focused on initiating positive impacts and leveraging The Herndon Home as a beacon of inspiration — re-opening the museum for tours and expanding programs that serve schools, scholars, and the community.
