The Herndon Family

Four Lives, One Enduring Legacy

The Herndons were one of the most prominent Black families in Atlanta's history — entrepreneurs, artists, educators, and philanthropists who shaped a city and a nation.

Alonzo Herndon

Founder of Atlanta Life Insurance Company. Born into slavery, he became one of America's first Black millionaires.

Adrienne Herndon

Actress, professor, and activist who designed this remarkable home in the early 1900s.

Norris B. Herndon

Expanded Atlanta Life globally and founded The Herndon Foundation in 1952.

Jessie Herndon

Preserved the family's legacy and championed education and community service.

Object of the Month

Hand-Carved Staircase Newel

This exquisite newel post, carved by an unnamed African American craftsman circa 1909, showcases the Renaissance Revival style Adrienne Herndon envisioned for the grand reception hall.

Each swirl and acanthus leaf was chiseled by hand — a testament to skill, pride, and artistry that visitors can still touch today.
